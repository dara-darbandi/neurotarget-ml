This project focuses on building a reproducible cheminformatics and machine-learning pipeline for analyzing neurotransmitters and neuroactive molecules. The initial scope includes collecting and cleaning molecular SMILES data, validating structures with RDKit, converting them into canonical SMILES, calculating basic molecular descriptors such as molecular weight and LogP, and preparing consistent datasets for later modeling. The project will then progress toward molecular fingerprint generation, similarity analysis, visualization, and baseline machine-learning models for predicting properties or biological activity related to neurological targets. The main goal is to develop a clean, modular, and testable foundation that can later support more advanced approaches such as multi-target prediction and graph neural networks.
